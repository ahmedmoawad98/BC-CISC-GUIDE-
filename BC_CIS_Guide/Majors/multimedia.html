Multimedia Computing

Whose should pursue a Multimedia Computing Degree?

Students who are interested in the types of computing that are used for entertainment, art and aesthetics. The major melds work in the Arts, Mathematics and Computer Science to produce students who are comfortable applying computing to non-traditional applications such as robotics, computer gaming, web production, and arts production.

Helpful tips

  • Students must complete Brooklyn College General Pathway Requirements along with the Multimedia Computing major courses to complete their Bachelors of Science Degree in Multimedia Computing.
  • To enroll in a CIS course, a student must have passed all prerequisite courses in the CIS department with a grade of C or higher.
  • To enroll in a Math course, a student must have passed all Math prerequisite courses with a D- or better.

Suggested Schedule and Flowchart

Possible Schedules

*In the schedules below, a CIS MMC elective is an additional course chosen from among CISC CISC 3140, 3150, 3410, 3415, 3610, 3650, 3660, 3665, 3810, 4610

Four Year Schedule:

This schedule is recommended for students who are Freshman. They need to combine two or three pathway courses along with this schedule per semester.

  • First semester: CISC 1115, CISC 1600, ENGL 1010, MATH 1011
  • Second semester: MATH 1201, ENGL 1012
  • Third semester: CISC 2820W, CISC 3115, MATH 1206
  • Fourth semester: CISC 2210, arts elective
  • Fifth semester: CISC 3130; CISC 3630, MATH 2501
  • Sixth semester: CISC 3220, CISC 3650 or 3660 or 3665, arts elective
  • Seventh semester: CISC 3620, MMC elective, CISC 4900 or 5001
  • Eighth semester: CIS MMC elective; CISC 4905 or CISC 5002 (optional)

Three Year Schedule:

This schedule is recommended for students who decided to major in Multimedia Computing in their second year. They need to combine two or three pathway courses along with this schedule per semester.

  • Third semester: CISC 1600, MATH 1011, arts elective
  • Fourth semester: CISC 1115, MATH 1201, arts elective
  • Fifth semester: CISC 2210, CISC 2820W, CISC 3115, MATH 1206
  • Sixth semester: CISC 3630, MATH 2501
  • Seventh semester: CISC 3130, CISC 3650 or 3660 or 3665; CIS MMC elective
  • Eighth semester: CISC 3220, CISC 3620, CIS MMC elective; CISC 4900 or 5001
