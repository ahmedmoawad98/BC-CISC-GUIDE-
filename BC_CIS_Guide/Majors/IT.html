Information Systems

Whose should pursue an Information Systems Degree?

Recommended for students who want to use their computing skills in the field of business and organizational management. Students will be prepared for careers as Business Analyst, Systems Analyst, Database Administrator, Information Technology Specialist, and Information Consultant.

Helpful tips

  • Students must complete Brooklyn College General Pathway Requirements along with the Information Systems major courses to complete their Bachelors of Science Degree in Information Systems.
  • To enroll in a CIS course, a student must have passed all prerequisite courses in the CIS department with a grade of C or higher.
  • To enroll in a Math course, a student must have passed all Math prerequisite courses with a D- or better.
  • Students aiming for this degree must complete 24 credits in advanced courses in the Departments of Accounting, CIS, Economics, and/or Finance and Business Management with a grade of C or higher.

Suggested Schedule and Flowchart

Possible Schedules

*In the schedules below, a Information Systems elective is CISC 3800 or 3120 or 3140 or 3171 or 3410 or 3820 or 3340 or 3345.

Four Year Schedule:

This schedule is recommended for students who are Freshman. They need to combine two or three pathway courses along with this schedule per semester.

  • First semester: CISC 1115, CISC 1050, MATH 1011, ENGL 1010
  • Second semester: CISC 1530, CISC 3110, BUS 2100, ENGL 1012
  • Third semester: CISC 2820W, CISC 3130, BUS 2200, BUS 3200
  • Fourth semester: CISC 1590, CISC 3810, CISC 3142, BUS 3400
  • Fifth semester: CISC 2531; CISC 3150; one CIS IS elective
  • Sixth semester: one or more CIS IS electives; BUS 3410
  • Seventh semester: CISC 1580W; CISC 4900 or CISC 5001; ACCT 2001
  • Eighth semester: CISC 4905 or CISC 5002, additional CIS or Business electives, BUS 3310

Three Year Schedule:

This schedule is recommended for students who decided to major in Information Systems in their second year. They need to combine two or three pathway courses along with this schedule per semester.

  • Third semester: CISC 1115, CISC 1050, MATH 1011, BUS 3200
  • Fourth semester: CISC 1530, CISC 1590, CISC 3115, BUS 2100
  • Fifth semester: CISC 2820W, CISC 3130, BUS 2200, BUS 3400
  • Sixth semester: CISC 2531, CISC 3810, one CIS IS elective, BUS 3410
  • Seventh semester: CISC 3150; one or more CIS IS electives; ACCT 2001
  • Eighth semester: CISC 1580W; CISC 4900 or CISC 5001; BUS 3310

Two-and-One-Half Year Schedule:

This schedule is only recommended for students who have taken CISC 1115 in their fourth semester. It would be very difficult to manage classes afterwards since students would have to take summer or winter classes in order to graduate with this degree on time. Students are encouraged to see a counselor before undertaking this schedule.

  • Fourth semester: CISC 1115, CISC 1050, MATH 1011, BUS 3200
  • Fifth semester: CISC 1530, CISC 1590, CISC 3115, BUS 2100, ACCT 2001
  • Sixth semester: CISC 2820W, CISC 3130, BUS 2200, BUS 3400
  • Seventh semester: CISC 2531, CISC 3810, one CIS IS elective, BUS 3410, BUS 3310
  • Eighth semester: CISC 3150, one CIS IS elective, CISC 1580W; CISC 4900 or 5001
