Computer Science Careers

1. Software Developer

  • Description: Software developers conceive of, design, and build computer programs. Some develop new applications for mobile or desktop use, while others build underlying operating systems. Either way, software developers identify user needs, build programs, test out new software, and make improvements. Working closely with computer programmers, software developers fill crucial roles in the computer systems, manufacturing, finance, and software publishing industries.
  • Entry-Level Salary: $61,140
  • Skills:
    1. ASP.NET
    2. JavaScript
    3. Java
    4. C#
    5. Python
  • Daily Task:
    1. Use various source debuggers and visual development environments to modify, write, and debug software for client applications.
    2. Document and test client software and write code to create applications that either stand alone or boost access to servers and services.
  • Education: Software developers typically need a bachelor’s degree in software engineering, computer science, or a similar field. Some of the senior-level roles may necessitate a master’s degree.

2. Software Test Engineer

  • Description: The role of a software test engineer is to coordinate the process for analyzing software programs. This process will involve creating and implementing testing methods, recording the test results, and providing recommendations to improve software programs based on the results.
  • Entry-Level Salary: $72,373
  • Skills:
    1. DevOps & Agile Methodology
    2. Automation
    3. Web & Mobile Technologies, and SDLC
  • Daily Task:
    1. Analyzing users stories and/use cases/requirements for validity and feasibility
    2. Collaborate closely with other team members and departments
    3. Execute all levels of testing (System, Integration, and Regression)
  • Education: As a software testing engineer, you will need a bachelor's degree. Suitable fields of study for this career will include computer science, software engineering, computer information systems, or information technology.

3. Systems Manager

  • Description: Computer and information systems managers generally oversee the information technology departments within businesses and organizations. A systems manager’s duties depend on organization size and how much technology they use on a daily basis. In smaller settings, systems managers may offer support on an as-needed basis, while larger organizations may require larger IT departments with more hands-on systems manager roles.
  • Entry-Level Salary: $63,495
  • Skills:
    1. Network Management
    2. IT Support
    3. IT Management
    4. Microsoft Office and Project Management
  • Daily Task:
    1. Computer and information systems managers assess organizational computer and technology needs, installing and maintaining software and hardware.
    2. Written and verbal communication skills enable systems managers to report on technological improvements and changes while informing colleagues about upgrades, information security, and related matters.
  • Education: Generally, systems managers hold at least a bachelor’s degree. Graduate education can increase earning potential and even open up paths to new and more advanced careers. Fortunately, the BLS projects careers in computer and information systems management to grow at an above-average rate of 11% in the coming years, making now a great time to kickstart a career in this rapidly growing industry.

4. Software Architect

  • Description: Software application architecture is the process of defining a structured solution that meets all of the technical and operational requirements, while optimizing common quality attributes such as performance, security, and manageability.
  • Entry-Level Salary: $87,027
  • Skills:
    1. Broad and extensive knowledge of the software development process and its technologies
    2. Knowledge of architectural styles and design patterns
    3. Understanding of various coding methods and computer languages
    4. Data modeling and database design experience
    5. Experience with software development lifecycle (SDLC) processes
    6. Experience with service oriented architecture (SOA)
  • Daily Task:
    1. Identifying business requirements and requirements of the stakeholders on the project
    2. Designing the entire system based on the received requirements
    3. Choosing the system architecture and each individual component of this system at a high level
    4. Choosing the technologies for the implementation of each component and connections between the components
    5. Architectural review
  • Education: Usually, software architects will have at least a bachelor's degree in computer science, information systems, software engineering or another related field.

5. Health Information Technician

  • Description: As a subset of the larger information technology field, health information technology incorporates interdisciplinary competencies from health and computer science. Health information technology professionals work in a variety of settings, including hospitals, insurance companies, and government agencies, collecting, interpreting, and protecting patient-related data.
  • Entry-Level Salary: $46,097
  • Skills:
    1. Medical Dat Entry
    2. Computer Software Support
    3. Attention To Detail
    4. Regulatory Compliance
    5. Interpersonal Communication
  • Daily Task:
    1. Health information technicians work with documents related to patient health.
    2. As medical coders, health information software specialists, and healthcare professionals, health information technicians review medical records and organize data while checking it for accuracy and clarity.
  • Education: With a bachelor’s degree, learners can advance to managerial and administrative roles while a master’s degree leads to executive positions. Students pursuing a master’s degree in health information technology study leadership, information management, and healthcare informatics.

Information Systems Careers

1. Database Administration

  • Description: Database administrators or managers create and maintain databases compatible with their companies’ needs. These information technology (IT) professionals oversee database updates, storage, security, and troubleshooting.
  • Entry-Level Salary: $53,292
  • Skills:
    1. SQL
    2. UNIX
    3. Linux
    4. Oracle
    5. Windows Operating System
    6. Microsoft Access
    7. HTML
  • Daily Task:
    1. Once they have determined user needs and set up databases with appropriate disk space, network requirements, and memory, database administrators may spend their days using software tools to organize and store company records, user information, and other data.
    2. Other daily tasks include upgrading database servers and applications, modifying database structure as needed, generating user profiles, and monitoring database security.
  • Education: IT jobs rely heavily on hard skills, which candidates can build through self-study and online learning tools. Still, most positions require an IT- or CS-related bachelor’s degree, as well. Some schools offer IT bachelor’s degree programs with concentrations in database administration or management.

2. IT Architect

  • Description: An information technology architect, also known as an enterprise architect, organizes and manages the security, communication, and related technological components within organizations and companies. Information technology architects provide oversight while ensuring compliance with rules and regulations, a position that requires advanced education and career experience.
  • Entry-Level Salary: $76,572
  • Skills:
    1. Information technology architects develop essential hard skills through education, training, and experience.
    2. Technical skills in systems architecture, virtualization, and cloud computing accompany security and infrastructure knowledge.
    3. Security, one of these professionals’ key duties, requires an understanding of computer systems, structures, and applications.
    4. Information technology architects also need managerial skills to prioritize and allocate needs and resources.
  • Daily Task:
    1. By creating plans and layouts for data communication networks, information technology architects serve as leaders and support staff alike.
    2. Information technology architects design, implement, and oversee security and business technologies for businesses and organizations.
    3. Information technology architects ensure efficiency and efficacy by communicating with colleagues and team members about software, network, and database architectures, as appropriate.
  • Education: Information technology architects must hold at least a bachelor’s degree in a computer-related field and five years of professional experience; some employers require candidates to hold a master’s degree.

3. Business Analyst

  • Description: Business Analysts conduct market analyses, analysing both product lines and the overall profitability of the business. In addition, they develop and monitor data quality metrics and ensure business data and reporting needs are met. Strong technology, analytical and communication skills are must-have traits.
  • Entry-Level Salary: $85,892
  • Skills:
    1. Gap Analysis
    2. Consulting Experience
    3. Test Cases
    4. SDLC
    5. Continuous Improvement
    6. CRM
    7. Microsoft SQL Server
  • Daily Task:
    1. Creating a detailed business analysis, outlining problems, opportunities and solutions for a business
    2. Budgeting and forecasting
    3. Planning and monitoring
    4. Variance analysis
    5. Pricing
    6. Reporting
  • Education: A bachelor's degree is standard, and occasionally employers prefer candidates with a master's degree. Relevant degrees include business administration, business, management, accounting, marketing, economics, statistics, and computer and information science.

4. Network Administration

  • Description: Network administrators design, manage, and maintain technological networks. They work within organizations and government agencies to oversee local area networks, wide area networks, network segments, and other data communication systems as needed.
  • Salary: $48,899
  • Skills:
    1. Local Area Network
    2. Wide Area Network
    3. Virtual Private Network
    4. Cisco Networking
    5. Microsoft Active Directory
  • Daily Task:
    1. Network administrators employ both soft and hard skills as they carry out daily tasks and responsibilities.
    2. Effective communication allows network administrators to exchange ideas with colleagues, assessing overall technological needs.
    3. Network administrators also train users in hardware and software, communicating complex ideas in clear, concise ways.
  • Education: Network administrators typically hold at least a bachelor’s degree. However, some professionals possess only a minimal amount of formal education beyond high school, while others hold graduate degrees in technology fields.

5. Security Analyst

  • Description: It is an incredibly important field in today’s business environment, since most organizations rely heavily on information systems in their daily work. Information security analysts work to secure information networks and systems in many different types of organizations. They protect computer networks by monitoring for and responding to threats.
  • Salary: $59,073
  • Skills:
    1. Tableau Software
    2. Cybersecurity
    3. Network Security Management
    4. Security Testing and Auditing
    5. Project Management
    6. Security Risk Management
  • Daily Task:
    1. The daily tasks of a cybersecurity worker vary depending on their specialty and employer.
    2. Information security analysts at banks typically work to secure financial transactions, while those working at technology companies may spend time protecting sensitive user data.
  • Education: Information security analyst education requirements typically include a bachelor’s degree in a field such as information assurance, information technology, or computer science.

Multimedia Computing Careers

1. Graphic Designer

  • Description: Graphic designers use color, illustrations, fonts, and layout to visually communicate a message or present a product. They design logos, product packaging, print materials, and websites, among many other things. Graphic designers are found in a variety of industries and in different capacities.
  • Entry-Level Salary: $39,316
  • Skills:
    1. Adobe Photoshop
    2. Adobe Illustrator
    3. Adobe InDesign
  • Daily Task:
    1. Create designs, concepts, and sample layouts, based on knowledge of layout principles and esthetic design concepts.
    2. Develop graphics and layouts for product illustrations, company logos, and Web sites.
    3. Use computer software to generate new images. Review final layouts and suggest improvements as needed.
    4. Education: For most entry-level and advanced graphic design positions, you will need a minimum of a bachelor's degree from an accredited university, college, or private design school.

2. Video Game Designer

  • Description: Video game designers develop user-friendly games for computers, mobile devices, and websites by brainstorming game details, building code, and testing products for efficiency. These individuals may create plots for games and organize ideas on flowcharts. Candidates must also solve problems that surface in early game drafts for usability and professional quality.
  • Entry-Level Salary: $50,000
  • Skills:
    1. Candidates with video game design degrees can develop technological skills for constructing prototypes and final products that are professional, user-friendly, and entertaining.
    2. Programs often require coursework that reflects these tasks, such as player experience, game mechanics, and computer-human interaction.
    3. These skills apply to a variety of careers where individuals consider audience and purpose to construct effective media, like software and websites.
  • Daily Task:
    1. rofessional responsibilities for video game designers include developing characters’ stories and traits, game plots, settings, and game rules.
    2. Candidates may also create obstacles within games, such as puzzles, and advise other professionals involved in the game’s design on ways of improving game quality.
    3. Individuals must adapt game details as needed for playability and consumer appeal.
    4. To perform these tasks, designers should boast strong technological skills to design graphics or develop code.Designers must also discuss project goals with clients and incorporate those concepts into games.
  • Education: Many careers related to video game design require each candidate to obtain at least a bachelor’s degree in a related field, such as computer science, computer graphics, software engineering, or game design.

  • Description As a multimedia programmer, your job is to design and create computer software that includes creative or interactive elements, such as additional graphics or audio.:
  • Entry-Level Salary: $45,013
  • Skills:
    1. Interactive design skills, such as user/task analysis and interface design/evaluation

  • Daily Task:

4. Film and video editor

  • Description: Responsible for cutting and editing film footage until it meets a director's approval. Rearranges and splices scenes that have already been shot, determines if scenes need to be reshot, and inserts music, sound, or optical effects.
  • Entry-Level Salary: $38,920
  • Skills:
    1. Attention to detail
    2. Adaptability
    3. Self-motivation
    4. Organizational skills
    5. Communication skills
  • Daily Task:
    1. Edit film until it meets director's specifications
    2. Watch scenes and determine which will end up on cutting room floor
    3. Rearrange scenes
    4. Work with and manipulate raw camera footage, dialogue, sound effects, graphics and special effects
    5. Determine which camera angles to use
    6. Insert music, sound, or optical effects
    7. Use nonlinear digital editing systems to edit clips
  • Education: There is not a specific degree required of film and video editors. However, most film and video editors have a bachelor’s degree or some college experience.
